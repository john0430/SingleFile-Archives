Removing my site from Google search
從Google搜尋中移除我的網站

2021-10-03

At this point, we’re slowly starting to understand the threat that surveillance capitalism poses to democracy and human autonomy. With your phone’s operating system being run by Google, your every question answered by Google Search, and Google scripts on every website you visit, it’s difficult to overstate how intimately the company knows you. This knowledge is then squeezed to extract the maximum possible profit — not just by showing you a “relevant ad” above a search result, but by modifying your off-screen behaviour. Nudging us to consume more is only the most banal example of a process and a power that’s fundamentally antithetical to individual self-determination1.
在這一點上，我們慢慢開始理解監控資本主義對民主和人類自主構成的威脅。由於您手機的作系統由Google運行，您的每個問題都由Google搜尋回答，並且您訪問的每個網站上的Google腳本都值得誇大，因此很難誇大公司對您的瞭解程度。然後，這些知識被擠壓以提取最大的利潤——不僅僅是通過在搜尋結果上方向您展示 「相關廣告」，而是通過修改您的螢幕外行為。推動我們消費更多隻是一個過程和權力的最平庸的例子，它與個人自決權根本背道而馳1。

Quoting from Shoshana Zuboff’s The Age of Surveillance Capitalism:
引用 Shoshana Zuboff 的《監控資本主義時代》：

…the essence of the exploitation here is the rendering of our lives as behavioral data for the sake of other’s improved control of us. The remarkable questions here concern the facts that our lives are rendered as behavioral data in the first place; that ignorance is a condition of this ubiquitous rendition; that decision rights vanish before one even knows that there is a decision to make; that there are consequences to this diminishment of rights that we can neither see nor foretell; that there is no exit, no voice, and no loyalty, only helplessness, resignation, and psychic numbing; and that encryption is the only positive action left to discuss when we sit around the dinner table and casually ponder how to hide from the forces that hide from us.
…這裡剝削的本質是將我們的生活渲染為行為數據，以便他人更好地控制我們。這裡引人注目的問題是，我們的生活首先被渲染為行為數據;無知是這種無處不在的演繹的一個條件;這個決定權甚至在一個人知道要做出決定之前就消失了;這種權利的減少會產生我們無法看到或預測的後果;沒有出口，沒有聲音，沒有忠誠，只有無助、順從和心靈上的麻木;而加密是當我們圍坐在餐桌旁，隨意思考如何躲避那些躲避那些躲避那些躲避我們的力量時，唯一需要討論的積極行動。

Here’s one more positive action: we can opt out. Perhaps not fully — e.g. refusing to use Google Meet for a job interview won’t leave a great impression — but we can stop providing Google with the content that they depend on.
還有一個積極的行動：我們可以退出宣告。也許不完全——例如，拒絕使用 Google Meet 進行求職面試不會給人留下很好的印象——但我們可以停止向 Google 提供他們所依賴的內容。

I don’t want to contribute to Google’s monopoly on search, or their overall creepiness. Granted, this website doesn’t get particularly large amounts of traffic, but if I can make Google Search a little bit less useful then it’s worth doing. This meta tag will block only Google’s web crawlers2:
我不想為Google對搜尋的壟斷或他們的整體令人毛骨悚然做出貢獻。誠然，這個網站並沒有獲得特別大的流量，但如果我能讓Google搜尋的用處降低一點，那就值得這樣做了。此 meta 標記將僅阻止 Google 的網頁爬蟲2：

<meta name="googlebot" content="noindex" />

I’ve added it to this website. It’ll take some time before it disappears from Google, and it’s only a tiny drop in the ocean, but a tiny drop is better than none.
我已將其加入到此網站。它從Google上消失需要一些時間，而且這隻是滄海一粟，但一滴總比沒有好。

If you’d like to join in the growing chorus of voices saying fuck off, Google, I recommend privacyguides.org as a starting point.
如果你想加入越來越多的聲音大合唱，說 Google 去他媽的，我推薦 privacyguides.org 作為起點。
